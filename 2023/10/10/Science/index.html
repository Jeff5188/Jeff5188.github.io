How STEM and science were an important part of my life

USACO score USACO account

Since I was young, I’ve been really into robotics, which is what sparked my interest in engineering and computer science. I started off by diving deep into robotics, taking part in several prestigious competitions that allowed me to apply my knowledge into action. I attended the John Hopkins Game design course and finished my final project and getting the CTY high honors. In middle school, I competed in the ACSL math competition. This offered me some valuable experience and made me even more interested as I had hands-on experiences with design, electronics, and programming. As my interest continued to grow, I joined the VEX Robotics Competition that is a part of the CREATE US Open robotics tournament. Being the team captain in this competition allowed me to practice my leadership skills as so I led strategic design decisions, solved mechanical issues, and refined the software. Eventually, our hard work paid off when our robot placed 2nd regionally. Throughout the school years, I also attended seminars and courses at the Shanghai Science Association to further enrich my robotics skills. One highlight of my robotics journey was when I wrote a research paper on optimizing the algorithms for intelligent car navigation. I explored adjusting the proportional–integral–derivative controller to enable smoother turning and less accidents. My paper was well-received by professors, validating my growth as a robotics engineer. Through a combination of competitions, courses, and research, I have developed advanced skills and a firm passion for robotics, making me eager to apply my knowledge in high school and beyond.

Representing the school to go to science fair

It was an honor to represent Yk Pao school School at the prestigious Shanghai Maker Fair. As a member of the school’s robotics club, I saw it as an opportunity to showcase my intelligent car project.
Over the past year, I designed and programmed an autonomous robot that worked through my improved A* algorithm. I implemented sensors and coding to allow it to navigate independently.
At the Maker Fair, I demonstrated our robot’s abilities to crowds. I especially enjoyed interacting with the children and sparking their curiosity about STEM. It was very rewarding to see the interest we generated and to represent all that Yk Pao students can achieve through hands-on learning and innovation. The event was a wonderful platform to share our passion and inspire others.
