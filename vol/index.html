COMMUNITY AND VOLUNTEER

video of volunteering at a Poorer region in Guangxi

During the summer after seventh grade, I had the incredible opportunity to volunteer at an English camp for middle school students in Guangxi Province, China. Guangxi is one of the less economically developed provinces in China, and many students there do not have the same access to English language education as students in bigger cities. For two years straight, I persisted to going to this camp and taught children in poverty ESL.

Teaching Image School Image

Our goal at the camp was not just to improve the students’ English skills through memorization and drills, but also to foster their love and enthusiasm for learning the language. I designed interactive lessons and activities to keep the students engaged and having fun while practicing English. One of the most popular activities was playing games like Hangman and crossword puzzles. Whenever we broke out the games, the energy in the classroom would instantly lift as the students began competing in small groups to solve the puzzles or guess the words before their classmates. You could see the smiles and laughs as they worked together in English to solve the puzzles and mysteries.

Certificate Certificate

The gratitude and enthusiasm from the students was extremely rewarding. At the closing ceremony, many of them expressed how much fun they had learning English in a relaxed, playful environment. Witnessing their progress and love of learning grow over just one week was incredibly inspiring. I’m thankful for the opportunity I had to volunteer and hope to continue finding ways to support education in underserved communities through fun and creative teaching.
